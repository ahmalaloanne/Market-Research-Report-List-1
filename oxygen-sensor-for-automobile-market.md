<p><p>The oxygen sensor in automobiles monitors exhaust oxygen levels to optimize fuel efficiency and reduce emissions. Zirconium Dioxide sensors are the most common, using a ceramic element to generate voltage based on oxygen concentration. They are durable and widely used. Titanium Dioxide sensors, less common, measure oxygen by changing electrical resistance. Both types ensure accurate air-fuel ratio control, but Zirconium sensors dominate due to their reliability and cost-effectiveness, while Titanium sensors are valued for their compact design and specific performance in certain applications.</p></p>
